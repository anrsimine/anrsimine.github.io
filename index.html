About

Junjian Wei is an undergraduate student majoring in Software Engineering at the School of Computer Science, Central China Normal University (CCNU), Wuhan, China. His research centers on robotic grasping in unstructured environments, with a focus on perception-guided grasp planning, affordance reasoning, geometric understanding, and reliable manipulation execution.

He is particularly interested in combining robust software engineering methodologies with embodied AI to develop reproducible, modular robotic systems capable of stable object grasping and manipulation in complex, real-world scenes.

Research Interests

Robotic Grasping

Grasp detection, pose estimation, and execution stability for unknown objects in unstructured environments.

Robot Perception

Fusion of visual, depth, and geometric information for robotic manipulation tasks.

Manipulation Planning

Planning, evaluation, and closed-loop control from perception to executable actions.

Software Engineering for Robotics

Emphasis on reproducibility, modular system design, and reliable automation of experimental pipelines.
